The fix is applied. Here's the summary:

## Root Cause

CSS specificity tie. The delete button in `App.tsx:501` is rendered with classes `btn btn--ghost btn--sm offcanvas-session-row__delete`. Three CSS rules compete for the border:

| Selector | Specificity | What it sets |
|---|---|---|
| `.btn` (line 1009) | (0,0,1,0) | `border: 1px solid var(--border-strong)` — full shorthand |
| `.btn--ghost` (line 1057) | (0,0,1,0) | `border-color: var(--border)` — overrides just the color |
| `.offcanvas-session-row__delete` (was line 2899) | (0,0,1,0) | `border: none` — resets all sub-properties |

All three have **equal specificity**. While `.offcanvas-session-row__delete` appears last in the stylesheet (line 2899 vs 1009/1057), `border` is a shorthand property that decomposes into `border-width`, `border-style`, and `border-color` sub-properties. The cascade resolves at the sub-property level, and the interaction between `border: none`'s shorthand reset and `.btn--ghost`'s longhand `border-color` can leave a visible border depending on browser interpretation.

## Fix

Changed the selector from `.offcanvas-session-row__delete` to `.offcanvas-session-row__delete.btn` — a compound selector with specificity (0,0,2,0). This guarantees `border: none` always wins over both `.btn` (0,0,1,0) and `.btn--ghost` (0,0,1,0), regardless of shorthand/longhand cascade ordering.
